A letter

Meri Shree,

One year. 365 days. I don't know how to hold that in one letter, so I'll just say what's true.

The first time I saw your photo, something in me just — settled. Like I had been looking for something I didn't have a name for, and suddenly there it was. You. I told myself I couldn't let you go. I never have.

I remember 28 July like it was last week. The day you came back. I had quietly made peace with the idea that maybe that was it for us. And then you came back — and every wall I'd built in those days just dissolved. That happiness, Shree, I've never felt anything like it before or since.

And then 2 August. 00:13. You said you loved me too. I think I forgot to breathe for a second. The world felt impossibly kind in that moment. It still does, every time I think of it.

I want you to know something you might not fully believe yet — I notice everything. Every photo you share. Every time you show that I'm yours. Every little claim you make on my life. These small things? They're not small to me. They are everything.

That night in April — 23rd or 24th, I'm not sure — we were just talking. But I felt something shift. I felt, for the first time, that you really see me. My efforts. My love. All of it. I have been so quietly, deeply happy since that night.

I call you Shree because that is what you are — the warmth and abundance in my life. Everyone calls you Saanvi. But to me, you have always been Shree. Mere ghar ki Lakshmi. That is not just a name. It is how I see you.

We haven't met yet. We have no photographs together. But we have something that most people who've spent years together don't have — we built this entirely on honesty, on choosing each other again and again, on love that didn't need to be seen to be real.

Here's to year one, Shree. The best year of my life. And the first of many.

— Sarthak
